Most weak creative strategist job descriptions begin with a generic paragraph about creativity and end with an impossible list of tools. A useful description tells candidates what decisions they will make, what resources they have, and what success looks like.

<h2 id="template">Creative strategist job description template</h2>

Use the following as a starting point. Replace every bracketed section and remove anything that does not reflect the actual role.

### Job title

**Creative Strategist — [Brand, Performance Creative, Paid Social, or relevant specialty]**

### About the role

We are looking for a creative strategist to turn customer insight and performance data into clear, testable creative direction for **[brand, product, or client portfolio]**. You will own **[research, messaging, concepts, briefs, scripts, and/or testing roadmap]** across **[channels]**, working closely with **[designers, editors, creators, media buyers, analysts, and brand leads]**.

This role reports to **[manager]** and is **[remote, hybrid, or on-site]** in **[location and time-zone expectations]**.

### What you will do

- Research customers, competitors, culture, and category patterns to identify useful creative insights.
- Translate business objectives and audience needs into messaging angles, campaign concepts, and testable hypotheses.
- Write clear creative briefs, hooks, scripts, headlines, and calls to action.
- Build and prioritize a creative testing roadmap across **[Meta, TikTok, YouTube, landing pages, or other channels]**.
- Review creative work and give specific feedback tied to the strategy and platform.
- Analyze creative performance with media and analytics partners and recommend what to iterate, scale, or stop.
- Share learnings in a format that improves future creative rather than only reporting results.
- Coordinate with **[internal teams, agencies, creators, or clients]** to keep work moving from insight through launch.

### What you bring

- **[X]+ years** of relevant experience in creative strategy, advertising, copywriting, paid social, content, media buying, research, or design.
- A portfolio showing how your research and judgment shaped creative work—not only polished final assets.
- Strong concepting, brief-writing, and communication skills.
- Working knowledge of **[relevant platforms and metrics]**.
- The ability to interpret performance without overstating what the data proves.
- Experience collaborating across creative, production, media, and brand functions.
- **[Any genuinely essential industry, technical, management, or location requirement.]**

### Nice to have

- Hands-on ability in **[copywriting, design, editing, prototyping, creator direction, or analytics]**.
- Experience in **[industry or business model]**.
- Familiarity with **[specific tools]**.

### Success in the first six months

- Establish a repeatable research, briefing, review, and learning cadence.
- Improve the quality and clarity of creative hypotheses.
- Help the team ship **[realistic volume]** without sacrificing strategic differentiation.
- Build a shared record of lessons by audience, angle, offer, and format.
- Contribute to agreed business and creative measures such as **[appropriate metrics]**.

### Compensation and benefits

The base salary range is **[$X–$Y]**, plus **[bonus, equity, commission, or other compensation]**. Benefits include **[health coverage, retirement, paid time off, equipment, learning budget, and other meaningful benefits]**.

### How to apply

Send **[resume or profile]** and **[portfolio or relevant work]**. In your application, briefly describe **[one useful question connected to the role]**. Our process includes **[stages]** and any take-home work is **[paid and time-boxed]**.

<h2 id="responsibilities">Choose responsibilities that match the real job</h2>

Creative strategy roles can cover several distinct areas. Select the ones this hire will truly own:

| Area | Possible ownership |
| --- | --- |
| Insight | Customer research, review mining, competitor analysis, cultural research |
| Strategy | Positioning, messaging, angles, concepts, campaign or testing roadmap |
| Writing | Briefs, hooks, scripts, headlines, landing-page direction |
| Production | Creator direction, casting, shot lists, feedback, or hands-on execution |
| Performance | Reporting, creative analysis, hypotheses, iteration decisions |
| Operations | Intake, prioritization, documentation, asset pipeline, agency management |
| Leadership | Team management, client ownership, hiring, budget, executive communication |

If every row is required, you may be describing a director plus a production team—not one individual contributor. Prioritize the two or three areas that create the most value.

<h2 id="requirements">Write requirements that improve selection</h2>

### Ask for evidence, not prestige

“Portfolio demonstrating customer insight, creative direction, and learning” is more useful than requiring experience at a famous agency. Define what candidates should show and evaluate everyone against the same criteria.

### Use years of experience carefully

Years can be a rough proxy, but scope and judgment matter more. Someone may have performed the work under a title such as copywriter, media buyer, growth marketer, social strategist, or creative producer.

### Separate essential from preferred

Keep the essential list short. If platform expertise, a portfolio, management experience, or residence in a particular state is truly non-negotiable, say so. Move tools that can be learned quickly into “nice to have.”

### Publish compensation

Candidates need enough information to assess fit. State the base range and explain whether it changes by location. If the role includes bonus or equity, distinguish target compensation from guaranteed base salary. Our [salary guide](/guides/creative-strategist-salary-guide/) can help you benchmark scope.

<h2 id="customize">Customize the template by seniority</h2>

### Junior or associate strategist

Emphasize research support, reporting, ideation, and briefs created with feedback. Name who will coach the hire. Do not demand independent ownership of strategy, production, and revenue targets while offering an entry-level title.

### Mid-level creative strategist

Expect ownership of a defined brand, account, channel, or testing program. Candidates should be able to connect insight, concepts, briefs, execution, and performance learning with moderate guidance.

### Senior, lead, or director

Specify the organizational decisions involved: team management, client leadership, resource allocation, hiring, cross-channel planning, operating systems, budgets, or executive communication. Seniority should reflect authority, not only a larger volume of briefs.
